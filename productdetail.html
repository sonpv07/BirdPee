Product's Desciption

In the wild Cockatiel (Malay parrot) usually works in pairs or small groups to forage, their food is usually seeds and cereals and some local agricultural pests. Often Cockatiel lives on dead branches, next to rivers and streams… Seem as they prefer to operate near water sources. Most of Cockatiels love bathing. Also they work on the face soil to forage for food such as seeds, grass, leaves and bark (from all trees, shrubs and other vegetation). At the same time they also eat some other insects and larvae. Cockatiel is quite healthy and disease resistant however, should not be subjective about their diet. They like to climb, bite and gnaw on wooden branches should not keep them in one place. When rearing, metal cages should be used, cage size for each pair Cockatiel is 60cm long, 45cm wide, 40cm high. Standing bridges Hardwoods should be used. Wooden nesting box in size about 35cm length , width 20cm, height 16cm, hole about 7-8 cm in diameter, inside to sawdust. Should be cleaned and replaced clean water daily. Change the sand once a week, the food box, the pitcher and Utensils must be disinfected once a month. Spring and autumn, large temperature changes, pay attention to adjust the room temperature to prevent prevent the occurrence of disease. Keep warm in winter, the temperature in house no lower than 16 ℃. Summer temperatures are higher, usually above 30 ℃ need to strengthen ventilation. In Northern Australia the Cockatiel's breeding season starts from April to July, in the south is from August to December. Each nest from 1-7 eggs on average about 4. After the chicks hatch, the parents give them eat in the morning and evening. In captivity, the rate of rearing Cockatiel breeding success is quite low, usually around 12 months age they start to be fertile however age to start for breeding is from 15-18 months old. Right in this age many Cockatiel is still in the development stage, the reproductive system not fully developed, leading to some reproductive traits Usually, shrewd eggs. Their nest should be 35 x 20 x 18cm, hole diameter 6-7cm, each nest usually lays 4-6 eggs Incubation period is about 19-21 days, after hatching should feed the young soft form. Cockatiel has up to 18 different color variations, and original The cause of their color change is 1. The lack of pigment. Example: color yellow, white face, albino; 2. Pigment changes. Eg: Cinnamon, Waller, Perth and more surface silver; 3. Change the color. For example: Pat and pearl. There are basically the following types: Pat Cockatiel, Cinnamon cockatiel, Waller cockatiel, Albino cockatiel, Whiteface cockatiel, Pass Multi cockatiel, Platinum cockatiel, Emerald chukar tail parrot. Nutrition: the main diet of parrots when rearing The cage is still the same as in the wild, but with more vegetables green. Commercial parrot food is also a good choice.
